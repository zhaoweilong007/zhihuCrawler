 <p data-pid="mbHkOxeZ">以下全是日常所见所闻，并且在国内未曾学习到的。</p>
 <p data-pid="GVQ49sv_">1. No problem!</p>
 <p data-pid="Of37ymwE">在国内学到的是：</p>
 <p data-pid="7DPrA3Tl">-Could you help me with xxx?</p>
 <p data-pid="nFWZNaTx">-<b><u>No problem!</u></b></p>
 <p data-pid="Pxz_1F0P">而在这边听到的往往是：</p>
 <p data-pid="QpwJ2VS2">-Thank you!</p>
 <p data-pid="V017z5Dk">-<b><u>No problem.</u></b></p>
 <p data-pid="BeXT_X7U">有时甚至：</p>
 <p data-pid="vGOqUhKq">-Oh sorry!</p>
 <p data-pid="yJW8fjnD">-<b><u>No problem!</u></b></p>
 <p data-pid="MEE7MUQi">2. Go by</p>
 <p data-pid="ESwCgeJw">在课堂自我介绍时，有时会说中文名，然后英文名。Instead of saying "My Chinese name is xxx, and my English name is xxx.", 你可以这样说：</p>
 <p data-pid="UeBVOHFK">-My name is xxx（你的原名）, and I <b><u>go by</u></b> xxx(你的英文名).</p>
 <p data-pid="7O8onOSl">一些美国学生不乐意用自己的原名，而偏好昵称，也可以用<u>go by来</u>表达：</p>
 <p data-pid="vYKNrpGX">-My name is Catherine, and I <b><u>go by</u></b> Cat.</p>
 <p data-pid="ibAbQUeu">3. Appreciate it!</p>
 <p data-pid="-J0KK1AZ">每次从校车上下来时，大家都会礼貌地对司机表示感谢，有次看到四个小哥鱼贯而出，每个人表达感谢的语句都不同。除了常见的"Thank you very much!"，"Thanks a lot!"以外，我觉得最好听的就是<b><u>“Appreciate it!"</u></b></p>
 <p data-pid="grjsx3_t">4. Have a good one!</p>
 <p data-pid="2Buf7mws">美国人很喜欢互相道好，无论认识不认识。每次和人再见的时候（可能是下班，结完账离开超市，下课等等），往往会对同事/收银员/老师/...说声"Have a good day/night"之类的。有时下午5,6点，天还没黑，我总会把”Have a good night!"说成"Have a good day!"，后来发现，还有种更简易的表达，日夜均可使用，即<b>"<u>Have a good one</u>!"，</b>简单顺口，客套必备。</p>
 <p data-pid="jWLaIXBU">5.宾语前置（知友</p><a href="https://www.zhihu.com/people/16fe0261fa0e270d16a9b9d622cbc363" data-hash="16fe0261fa0e270d16a9b9d622cbc363" class="member_mention" data-editable="true" data-title="@Scrummble" data-hovercard="p$b$16fe0261fa0e270d16a9b9d622cbc363">@Scrummble</a>
 <p data-pid="pBpEB282">指出应该是状语前置）</p>
 <p data-pid="zGqRDLbq">这是种B格很高的表达方式，在每张美元背面都有一句话"<b><u>In God We Trust</u></b>"（如下图）</p>

 <br>
 <p data-pid="Er-z5ESq">6. Without further ado</p>
 <p data-pid="NW2bJam8">这句在YouTube视频里经常听到，那些YouTubers在视频开头往往先介绍这个视频是干什么的，再顺便扯几句近期生活，然后在切入正题之前，有时会用这么一句"<b><u>Without further ado</u></b>, let's get started."这句话的意思就类似于“<b><u>废话少说</u></b>，让我们开始吧。”</p>
 <p data-pid="A47OOtK9">每次听到这句，我都会联想另一个四字常用词汇“原来如此”，真希望它在英文里也有完全对应的翻译，我就不用每次都将说了半句的“搜迪斯卡”咽下去了。。。</p>
 <p data-pid="N2OcroQr">7. Figure</p>
 <p data-pid="fLH9mJAm">当想表达“我觉得/我认为/我猜”的时候，有个很好用的表达就是“I figure.."</p>
 <p data-pid="VAcchd-0">-<b><u>I figured</u></b> you might wanna be alone for a while, so I didn't bother you.</p>
 <p data-pid="3ScZGXSt">8. Petite/plus size/pale/fair/tan/dark</p>
 <p data-pid="P9udqpPg">这几个都是形容外貌的，就放在一起说。</p>
 <p data-pid="wZ8YX5HV">在形容人个子矮的时候，short可能不够委婉，一般说<b><u>petite</u></b>。</p>
 <p data-pid="Y95cffL8">在形容人胖的时候，fat明显不够委婉，可以说<b><u>plus size</u></b>。</p>
 <p data-pid="Wzq34-78">在形容肤色时，长得白和黑不是white和black（慎用！），而是<u><b>pale/fair</b></u>（形容白），<b><u>tan/dark</u></b>形容黑</p>
 <p data-pid="QzuykLqa">9. Email常用礼貌用语</p>
 <p data-pid="hYmW_8j9">-I hope this email finds you well.用于开头。</p>
 <p data-pid="sOowG2YS">-Any response will be appreciated.用于结尾，通常正文是找对方询问事情。</p>
 <p data-pid="GXVasNKq">-Please feel free to let me know if there should be any question.用于句尾，通常正文是上交文件，材料，或者回答问题等。</p>
 <p data-pid="MnyfgEWx">-It would be great/the best if you could...这就是传说中比"Could you please..."还要更加委婉的请求式句型了。</p>
 <p data-pid="v-qOVOUw">10. I'm good.</p>
 <p data-pid="lSzylY3P">这句可以用来回答How are you?此外，常用于：</p>
 <p data-pid="ZId-2xNk">-Do you want some chips with your sandwich?</p>
 <p data-pid="X2plf3t6">-No, <u><b>I'm good</b></u>. Thanks.</p>
 <p data-pid="ApS2DuOu">-Do you have any questions?</p>
 <p data-pid="SFD3--Cr">-No, <u><b>I'm good</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="WhyoAEZu">也可用作委婉拒绝。</p>
 <p data-pid="IZ2dqSuN">-Do you wanna go to a strip club?</p>
 <p data-pid="QhX6BwaU">-<b><u>I'm good</u></b>.</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="Yff8pU-g"><u><b><i>（以下为8.2更新）</i></b></u></p>
 <p data-pid="ejdZ47N8">11. Shoot</p>
 <p data-pid="VzeVcCAR">除了“射击”之意，还有以下日常用法：</p>
 <p data-pid="UuRADjUu">-Whenever you need help, just <u>shoot</u> me an email. （比send更口语化）</p>
 <p data-pid="yXFTkI4j">-I've collected some negative comments on you.</p>
 <p data-pid="fMIFxTwr">-<b><u>Shoot</u></b>. （类似于Go ahead说吧)</p>
 <p data-pid="Pp7bjstG">-I was <u><b>shooting</b></u> for 100, but 98 is ok. （意为本想<b>冲击</b>100分）</p>
 <p data-pid="UWrcgP06">-Oh <b><u>shoot</u></b>...（其实就是Oh s**t! 的委婉说法）</p>
 <p data-pid="RDb11Y_t">12. 结束对话用语</p>
 <p data-pid="N3NEgptQ">-I'll leave you be. （类似于“打扰了”，“告辞了”，是在《纸牌屋》里看到Claire对一个白宫工作人员或是记者说的）</p>
 <p data-pid="GC9Le2RB">-As you were. （这句通常上司对下属讲完工作之后可能用到，是在《基本演绎法》里看到的）</p>
 <p data-pid="Bk9mh2mN">13. Off the hook</p>
 <p data-pid="stMo7UF8">意为“从麻烦脱身”，可以想象一条鱼脱离鱼钩的样子。</p>
 <p data-pid="Yk4KucPQ">-He paid all the fines so he's finally <b><u>off the hook</u></b> now.</p>
 <p data-pid="sO-Jw9eO">-He's <u><b>off the hook</b></u>, he doesn't do drugs any more.</p>
 <p data-pid="OIpXSGBN">《绝望主妇》里面Bree问她儿子Andrew，现在年轻人还说"Awesome"吗？Andrew告诉她，他们现在都说"Off the hook"，可见，这个词组也有Cool, awesome之意。</p>
 <p data-pid="PwRaO22Q">-That party was <u><b>off the hook</b></u>!</p>
 <p data-pid="imvRINWq">印象中《宋飞正传》里Jerry, Elaine和George有对off the hook和on the hook的一段讨论，但是在网上搜出来的似乎都是小黄网站，于是隐隐觉得似乎这个表达不是什么好表达了。。。</p>
 <p data-pid="oqCGgHYW">14. Hands down</p>
 <p data-pid="H6zW75lx">有“绝对，毫无疑问”之意。可以这样联想，因为毫无疑问，所以不会举手提问，也就是hands down。</p>
 <p data-pid="lAACNMFa">-My favorite TV show is <u><b>hands down</b></u><i>Friends.</i></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="TQwDCR0b">15. Though</p>
 <p data-pid="e9ENQKvI">大家都熟悉though用于句首和句中表转折，但可能不太了解它放在句末的用法。</p>
 <p data-pid="v2Um6-x_">-Do you want me to get you a cup of coffee?</p>
 <p data-pid="P45d3iko">-No, I'm good. Thank you <u><b>though</b></u>. （意为<u><b>不过还是）</b></u></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="-9pbyRSY">另外一个用法是在Instagram上看到的，关注了一个账号整天推送搞笑短视频，内容都是人和动物摔倒啊，被砸啊，被吓到啊等等很囧的场面。然后这个账号经常配的文字就是以though结尾的语句，可以感觉到明显的<u>讥讽意味</u>。例如：</p>
 <p data-pid="NBC68_DP">（一个男人被电梯整蛊吓得尖叫）</p>
 <p data-pid="KSjV1Qdj">-That scream <b><u>thoug</u></b><u><b>h</b></u>. （重音在scream）</p>
 <p data-pid="K6Tmv0kd">(一只狗打了一个喷嚏，然后全身狂抖）</p>
 <p data-pid="DYDGNDUg">-That sneeze <b><u>though</u></b>.（重音在sneeze）</p>
 <p data-pid="UiZJfigw">16. Sure/Of course/Certainly</p>
 <p data-pid="VguT2ATR">当时初中学英语的时候记得这三个词经常一起出现，表达“当然”，一直模模糊糊地认为他们是一个意思，后来慢慢地才了解到他们的用法的区别。</p>
 <p data-pid="eaoz7WOO">Sure一般用来表达欣然同意对方的提议，意为“<b><u>好呀</u></b>”：</p>
 <p data-pid="TxHEN7Hm">-Can I give you a call?</p>
 <p data-pid="3ZhKaKey">-<b><u>Sure</u></b>!</p>
 <p data-pid="XKSEmN1e">-Would you like to get a cup of coffee or something?</p>
 <p data-pid="0RgEIGxL">-<b><u>Sure</u></b>!</p>
 <p data-pid="wWhNIfKK">而Of course则有<b><u>“那当然了”，“不用多说”</u></b>的意味在里面：</p>
 <p data-pid="ZfC0NWsV">-There are, <u><b>of course</b></u>, exceptions to the rule.</p>
 <p data-pid="AekmYfWh">-What do you do now?</p>
 <p data-pid="b2zoNo6o">-Still farming.</p>
 <p data-pid="iikXFkqT">-<u><b>Of course</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="fiyrgB05">（此对话来自《唐顿庄园》里庄主Robert问他领地里的一位农民现在做些什么，农民回答还是做农活，并且一脸颓丧的样子，潜台词是“我还能做啥，还不是做做农活而已”，Robert听后稍愣了一下，大概觉得自己问的问题有点愚蠢，于是说了句<u><b>Of course</b></u>. 可见，如果问了一个本该知道答案的问题，对方回答后，你可以说这么一句<u><b>Of course.</b></u>）</p>
 <p data-pid="yiTcpcxz">Certainly则比较少见，在Google上查到它的使用频率也是渐渐下降：</p>
 <figure>
  <img src="https://picx.zhimg.com/50/da6d8ed2fd1fb8c17dc4ff8c6fe89e3b_720w.jpg?source=1940ef5c" data-rawwidth="542" data-rawheight="190" data-original-token="da6d8ed2fd1fb8c17dc4ff8c6fe89e3b" class="origin_image zh-lightbox-thumb" width="542" data-original="https://pic1.zhimg.com/da6d8ed2fd1fb8c17dc4ff8c6fe89e3b_r.jpg?source=1940ef5c">
 </figure>
 <p data-pid="RgQ1O1EV">不知道是不是一个渐渐淡出江湖的词汇。</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="jDj7NVVk"><u><b><i>（以下为8.4更新）</i></b></u></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="OFcPn37e">17. 模糊语气</p>
 <p data-pid="e-jJIZyh">在表达“差不多”，“接近”，“...的样子” ，“之类的”等模糊的意思时，除了nearly, approximately, almost, kind of, sort of 等词汇外，更口语的说法还有<b><u>or s</u></b><b><u>o</u>, <u>-ish</u></b><b>, <u>...s</u></b><b><u>omething</u></b>等：</p>
 <p data-pid="4Z3udbon">-He has worked in the company for a year and two months <u><b>or so.</b></u></p>
 <p data-pid="ruSWwFTN">-After dinner I had 30 cherries <u><b>or so</b>.</u></p>
 <p data-pid="IK79Av8j">-That movie was good-<u><b>ish</b></u>.（此人应该觉得电影并不怎么好看）</p>
 <p data-pid="-iVr_2GA">-That color is blue-<u><b>ish</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="a4XhXawd">-Let's meet around 9-<u><b>ish.</b></u></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="x_aR3dfU">-30 books every <u><b>twenty</b></u><u><b>-something</b></u> girl must read. （类似于知乎豆瓣经常出现的<u><b>20多岁</b></u>女孩的必读书单）</p>
 <p data-pid="fV2BGC_I">-Wanna a cup of water or <u><b>something</b></u>?</p>
 <p data-pid="8MFrPQ3j">18. Sense</p>
 <p data-pid="MxoWpp8I">sense的意思非常多，有“...感”，“意思/意识”，“目的”等，无法和一个中文词汇完全对应。国内用到a sense of的标配经常是a sense of humor，其实可以使用的范围非常广。</p>
 <p data-pid="boC09K31">-What is <u><b>the sense of</b></u> going out in the rain? （意为“<u><b>目的”</b></u>）</p>
 <p data-pid="Jx4ivBjD">-The purpose of the meeting is to let you get <u><b>a sense of</b></u> how we work. （好难翻译！请意会）</p>
 <p data-pid="GAo0vuY0">-Does that <u><b>make sense</b></u> to you?（这句话很常见，通常在解释完某个复杂的事情或事物后，为了解对方是否听明白，会问这么一句，配以拧巴的眉头，微倾的头颅）</p>
 <p data-pid="hidrxu4G">19. 没听懂对方的话时</p>
 <p data-pid="lIlufOJm">听不懂或没听清对方的意思，这种状况常常发生，不论你是不是native speaker。除了Sorry? Excuse me? Could you repeat that? 以及英语课本里教的I beg your pardon? 可以用来要求对方再说一遍外，还可以这样告诉对方<u><b>你没听清</b></u>：</p>
 <p data-pid="I2bYfKRl">-!#%^*&gt;??#^&amp;</p>
 <p data-pid="ejeePSEn">-Sorry, <u><b>you lost me</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="iKyi98aL">-#%^&amp;^$(!_+?&gt;&lt;</p>
 <p data-pid="v-7GJ9WN">-Sorry, <u><b>I didn't follow</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="aLbzw266">20. A touch of</p>
 <p data-pid="hNcTcYzP">意为"<u><b>一点儿</b></u>"， 但是不是比a little, a bit of听起来要更有画面感？</p>
 <p data-pid="hdAL5YEA">-"Chinese White Tie" means White Tie with <u><b>a touch of </b></u>China. （这是美国Vogue杂志主编Anna Wintour给记者解释刚过去的中国主题Met Gala的dress code的含义时用的原句。）</p>
 <p data-pid="EODoOheA">-I have <u><b>a touch of</b></u> flu and need some rest.（意为“<u>轻微的</u>”）</p>
 <p data-pid="9Vw-6V6R">21. Shy of</p>
 <p data-pid="4bviqLDV">意思类似于short of。</p>
 <p data-pid="ydEMozNX">-He's just <u><b>shy of</b></u> 6 feet. （"<u><b>接近</b></u>"6英尺=1.8288米）</p>
 <p data-pid="oiRTxfJz">《老友记》里面有次6人玩扑克牌，Ross加注的时候发现钱不够了，说了句"Joey, I'm a little shy here."，意思是钱不够了，想找Joey借钱，结果Joey傻了吧唧地拍了拍Ross肩膀，说有哥们儿在，不怕。还好Chandler听出了shy的第二层意思，掏出了钱包。</p>
 <p data-pid="nTmJqpIM">22. Dress up/down</p>
 <p data-pid="uh31s6l1">Dress up可不是穿上衣服的意思，而是<b><u>穿着正式</u></b>的意思，相应的<b>穿着随意</b>则是dress down.</p>
 <p data-pid="7pAfOxYd">-You need to<b><u> dress up</u></b> for the dinner. But during the daytime, you can <b><u>dress down</u></b> as you like.</p>

 <br>
 <p data-pid="eo2qInG9">23. 又是几个对话常见表达，适用于特定场合。</p>
 <p data-pid="3Imw-Cao">在宾馆/图书馆/售票处/电话客服..办事结束后，经常会听到一句"<b><u>You're all set.</u></b>" 听到这句话就知道事儿已办妥可以拎着东西滚了。如果不知道自己的事儿是不是弄好了，而对方又专心致志作敲键盘状时，可以问一句"<b><u>Am I all set?</u></b>" 不要像楼主第一次住完宾馆在前台傻了吧唧地站在那儿等人家给我receipt等了二十分钟，人家终于忍不住问我"What else can I help you with?"才知道我早就可以滚了。。</p>
 <p data-pid="AqKk9pbz">有时用公共饮水机/洗手池时有可能不巧地前面正好有人在使用，于是站在别人身后两英尺处耐心等待，同时说一声"<b><u>Take your time</u></b>" 让对方不用太着急，对方使用完可能对你说声"<b><u>All yours</u></b>"让你放心上前使用，文明的世界真美好。</p>
 <p data-pid="y8MGySAX">24. At some point</p>
 <p data-pid="4sJlMn8D">这个表达非常好用，可以表示过去某时刻，也可以表示将来某时刻。</p>
 <p data-pid="elRf6Iy0">-<b><u>At some point</u></b> I decided she was no longer my friend. （表示之前某时刻）</p>
 <p data-pid="EDyRFNTY">-Do you want me to add that information in the document?</p>
 <p data-pid="LEvk5_Jr">-No. But <b><u>at some point</u></b> we will need that. （表示将来某时刻，比in the future明显更适用）</p>
 <p data-pid="3ijuombl">25. Comfort</p>
 <p data-pid="9zkzexij">Comfort的意思大家都懂，就是舒服，舒适，爽。在国内的时候，我只知道俺家的沙发是comfortable的，而在老美的口语表达中，这个词可以使用的语境实在是多。</p>
 <p data-pid="8WdZAaIE">-You should make sure you are <b><u>comfortable</u></b> with the height every time you jump off. （这是楼主第一次去攀岩俱乐部玩抱石时，教练对我说的，comfortable在这里的意思更贴近于“<b>没有不适，能接受”</b>）</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="TAtuvUCf">-If no support was provided, we should perform audit steps to obtain <b><u>comfort</u></b> that the item was appropriately included. （这里意译过来应该是<b>“确保”</b>，意思等同于make sure）</p>
 <p data-pid="jzh7xLug">可见comfortable这种状态，不一定是躺在沙发上全身放松，还可以是生活工作中每一个细小的，让你呼吸平稳，心跳规律的状态。</p>
 <p data-pid="dX7WwIVL"><i><u><b>（以下为8.25更新）</b></u></i></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="0d0-G4GS">26. Thing</p>
 <p data-pid="lmptLfI6">除了“事物”之意外，在口语中还有以下常见用法：</p>
 <p data-pid="nHe2YzS4">-I think he has <u><b>a thing</b></u> for you. （就是中文里“他对你<u>有意思”</u>的意思）</p>
 <p data-pid="sWvQIzwq">-Being funny is Chandler's <u><b>thing</b></u> and Ross's <u><b>thing</b></u> is getting divorced. (来自《老友记》，这里thing不好翻译，可以理解为“<u><b>某人最显著的特质</b></u>”，即让别人想到你就联想起来的东西。）</p>
 <p data-pid="aRWFvawo">27. Have/get your back</p>
 <p data-pid="3TZBjRC4">意为“<u><b>罩着你</b></u>”，“<u>挺你</u>”或者“<b><u>有我在</u></b>”。</p>
 <p data-pid="ygjXaLAb">-If you ever need help, just ask. You know I <u><b>have your back</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="_MwznA8m">-Just go ahead. Don't worry. <b><u>I got your back.</u></b></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="_BKwJ_nB">28. In the middle of nowhere</p>
 <p data-pid="UYwtlX4O">意为“<b><u>荒郊野外</u></b>”。</p>
 <p data-pid="3yVHHTeC">-Bree once dropped her teenage son <u><b>in the middle of nowhere</b></u>.</p>
 <p data-pid="S2BC8ycf">29. Walk through</p>
 <p data-pid="Nx-QPJMt">虽然这个词组里有walk这个动词，但是通常和行走没有太大关系。可以理解为“<b><u>过一遍</u></b>”。</p>
 <p data-pid="KEQ9w96h">-Could you <b><u>walk me through</u></b> the process so I can get a better understanding of your business?</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="IewjrLmi">30. 标点符号的说法</p>
 <p data-pid="rtlBSKj0">仔细想想我们还没有好好学习过标点符号的英语表达呢，那就一起总结一下吧！</p>
 <p data-pid="dLJj2ETJ">, comma</p>
 <p data-pid="qO7-qD3X">. period</p>
 <p data-pid="xWJRFF4r">— dash （破折号，用法为句子A - 句子B，注意字符两边都有空格）</p>
 <p data-pid="i91IZk79"># pound （#有时候可作编号之意，比如“#1”为“编号1”， 而No.1则为“排名第一”）</p>
 <p data-pid="wLu5Et5P">/ slash</p>
 <p data-pid="3J9P4Q-1">\ backslash</p>
 <p data-pid="k7MyXjYe">"" quote</p>
 <p data-pid="sQI1Da8O">() [] {} brackets</p>
 <p data-pid="8itfF7Yi">*star</p>
 <p data-pid="wlNzkph5">_ underscore</p>
 <p data-pid="72jxAMb4">- hyphen （连字符，用法为word-word，组成一个新词，例如anti-discrimination，字符两边没有空格）</p>
 <p data-pid="FuKFHsIt">~ tilde</p>
 <p data-pid="v2ib4cIX">! exclamation point/mark</p>
 <p data-pid="slnDBn_W">? question mark</p>
 <p data-pid="_eNKtlxV">' apostrophe</p>
 <p data-pid="U4q3g1Og">: colon</p>
 <p data-pid="Zd9Gtfma">; semicolon</p>
 <p data-pid="C7UcmIwl">• bullet</p>
 <p data-pid="ok-us9Zu">numerator 分子</p>
 <p data-pid="YM9PeYMh">denominator 分母</p>
 <p data-pid="T6nK60rR">（真是涨姿势啊！）</p>
 <p data-pid="FX7QzDHC"><u><b><i>（以下为11.21更新）</i></b></u></p>
 <p data-pid="8BM1-btv">31. Doesn't hurt to do something</p>
 <p data-pid="UM_evxlU">意为“做什么事有好处/不会有坏处”。</p>
 <p data-pid="HR-R7vap">-<b><u>It does't hurt to</u></b> take a look of what you've done and see if it could be improved.</p>
 <p data-pid="elbpoAi_">32. You might wanna/you don't wanna</p>
 <p data-pid="YVEzRwgT">这可以是给别人建议做某事/别做某事时使用的短语。</p>
 <p data-pid="TIwcbznJ">-<b><u>You might wanna</u></b> have a cup of water at hand in case that food is too spicy.</p>
 <p data-pid="VnZ1ap0-">-<b><u>You don't wanna</u></b> call her in the middle of the night. You don't know how grumpy she could be.</p>
 <p data-pid="u2W2_gyT">33. Can use some</p>
 <p data-pid="h1IWiXvI">意为“正需要”。</p>
 <p data-pid="HbC6S4x8">-After a long week, I <b><u>can really use some</u></b> rest.</p>
 <p data-pid="BxsHH6ch">-That shirt is too flashy.</p>
 <p data-pid="DIL5z7AZ">-You <b><u>can use some</u></b> flash.</p>
 <p data-pid="H0Pns8pR">（来自《宋飞正传》）</p>
 <p data-pid="MKi7AZhw">34. At the end of the day</p>
 <p data-pid="LC--1meS">意为“到头来”，“到最后”，“最终”，并不是“一天的最后”。</p>
 <p data-pid="TznjR3D5">-They all said they would help me, but <b><u>at the end of the day</u></b> nobody did.</p>
 <p data-pid="Wu54Tdp9">35. The last thing I wanna do</p>
 <p data-pid="nQ1tf4PF">可不是“最后一件想做的事”，而是“最不想做的事”。</p>
 <p data-pid="HjUPUNJN">-<b><u>The last thing I wanna do</u></b> is hurting your brother.</p>
 <p data-pid="lOai7RbA">（来自《老友记》Rachel对Monica说她不会伤害Ross）</p>
 <p data-pid="MEsVZP_v"><u><b><i>（以下为4.14.2016更新）</i></b></u></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="NK-wrsyF">36. Be my guest</p>
 <p data-pid="gFLPHcd3">这个表达可不是邀请对方做客，而是<b><u>在对方提出要做某件事的时候给出允许</u></b>。</p>
 <p data-pid="3Pscl-1a">-Can I have the last piece of cake?</p>
 <p data-pid="-2M7Oxil">-Sure! <b><u>Be my guest</u></b>.</p>
 <p data-pid="Aaz0aeKJ">老友记里面有一集Monica和Rachel楼上的邻居把地毯撤掉了，各种噪声通过天花板传来听得一清二楚，Pheobe问她们怎么不上去和那人说，Monica表示那人太帅了，她没法开口。Phoebe不信，决定要上楼去教训下那个人。Monica和Pheobe之间有这样一段对话：</p>
 <p data-pid="kLB8173K"><strong>MONICA :</strong> He took up the carpets and now you can hear everything.</p>
 <p data-pid="KEO6S0gI"><strong>PHOEBE :</strong> Well, why don’t you go up there and ask him to just, like "step lightly, please"?</p>
 <p data-pid="w_LVtbOu"><strong>MONICA :</strong> I have, like five times, but they guy is so charming that I go up there to yell and then I end up apologizing to him.</p>
 <p data-pid="qJXaFDMa"><strong>PHOEBE :</strong> Ewww, that is ...</p><em>(angrily)</em>
 <p data-pid="0b6-Xoy5">silly. I’ll go up there, I’ll tell him to keep it down.</p>
 <p data-pid="Lgvzz1oF"><strong>MONICA :</strong> Alright. <b><u>Be my guest. </u></b></p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="ks0x9zzq">37. Gut</p>
 <p data-pid="_Y2cdmtk">直译过来是“肚子”，但在英语里面有“<u><b>直觉</b></u>”之意。</p>
 <p data-pid="M3yB9AQv">-I don't know how to decide.</p>
 <p data-pid="Lb4NVRtN">-Well, what does your <u>gut</u> tell you?</p>
 <p data-pid="v6Tfd37k">有时又指“<b><u>胆量</u></b>”。</p>
 <p data-pid="FNaRcfeM">-I hate that guy! I want to punch him in the face!</p>
 <p data-pid="6e_Q2MGi">-You don't have the <b><u>guts</u></b>.</p>
 <p data-pid="AFcC11Nl">38. It works</p>
 <p data-pid="OYa8IXfM">这个表达在日常口语中实在太常见了。在国内时work往往指工作，但在英语中更像是说某件事“<b><u>有效运行</u></b>”。</p>
 <p data-pid="9BpRI3kC">-I don't understand how polygamy <b><u>works</u></b>.</p>
 <p data-pid="j3s19dRs">-We tried a new model and it <b><u>worked</u></b>!</p>
 <p data-pid="xg-dONvG">39. Buy that</p>
 <p data-pid="hCtXoPxt">除了“<b><u>买它</u></b>”的直译外，还有“<u>相信一个说法”</u>的意思。来看例句理解吧：</p>
 <p data-pid="7QRvgauZ">-He said he didn't do it but <b><u>I don't buy it</u></b>.</p>
 <p data-pid="Ly6wc1Cp">-She told me she missed our date because she had to work.</p>
 <p data-pid="6A_u453k">-That's so made up! I can't believe you <b><u>bought it</u></b>.</p>
 <p data-pid="wzdWhS7-">40. Table</p>
 <p data-pid="-ghBKKEv">几个和table有关的表达：Bring to the table和On/Off the table。</p>
 <p data-pid="7_uTtzH6">Bring to the table指“<b><u>带来的好处</u></b>”：</p>
 <p data-pid="tz5JKngP">-Do you have any advice for me on the interview?</p>
 <p data-pid="_tcLukh6">-Just be yourself and talk what you can <u>bring to the table</u> with all the experience you have.</p>
 <p data-pid="b6DmlI1B">On the table指“<b><u>考虑的</u></b>”，相应的，Off the table指“<b><u>不作考虑的</u></b>”：</p>
 <p data-pid="T2a0-EXO">-The proposed new bill will soon be <b><u>on the table</u></b> in Parliament.</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="ZRH563Cc">-We will take no options <u>off the table</u> to achieve that goal.</p>
 <br>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="g5ONDE5N">例句来自Google，众多美剧英剧，答主亲耳所闻，和现场脑补。</p>
 <p data-pid="oZoiYRuG">其余想起再更。</p>
 <br>
 <p data-pid="bB7Ed-WC">（深切地感觉到，语言的学习真是终生的，也是每一天的。来美一年，英语进步了，语文却因为读得少了，写得少了，退步很多，最明显的感受就是对成语的运用能力大大下降。语言不仅是表达方式，更是思维方式，一般人来回切换模式做不到那么干脆利落，所以常见海外党写起东西来中英夹杂。深有体会，但不该如此。生活多年的环境在自己身上打下的文化烙印值得慢慢品味，好好珍惜。）</p>
