# 在日常英语对话中有哪些细节中国人不会注意，但是外国人却觉得很重要，并可能认为中国人很无礼？
- 点赞数：11574
- 更新时间：2018年09月08日12时16分01秒
- 回答url：https://www.zhihu.com/question/30964795/answer/137634710
<body>
 <blockquote data-pid="ETebEHpf">
  <b>本文首发我的微信公众号「史蒂芬的专栏」（ID: stehouse)。</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="IlNDz-sL">我以前老板（美国人）调回美国总部工作，走之前还不忘告诉我们说任何话时，一定要用tentative的语气。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="pDKYSVOu">他还推荐了一本书《Crucial Conversations》，据说他的建议就是从这本书里来的。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="blKq0xbV">刚才找了一下，美亚上有英文原版，中亚上有有中文版《关键对话:如何高效能沟通》。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="u0aYMLCc"><b>风险提示： 我没看过这本书，不知写得好不好，但是毕竟是亚马逊 best-seller，应该不会很差。百度可以搜到到中文和英文版的pdf版。</b></p>

 </figure>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="Gp9sHLLc">所谓tentative语气，字面上的意思就是「试探性」的语气，也就是不要把话说死，给自己留一点余地。</p>
 <p data-pid="LAPs3rnl"><b>注意tentative 不等于 uncertain的语气，注意和maybe这种语气区分开来。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="fVizJNxG"><b>比如多用seem, appear, sound, look这样的词。</b></p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="UWS2epoS">
  例如. 
  <br>
  This is wrong 改成<b>This seems to be wrong.</b>
  <br>
  We don't have other option 改成 <b> It seems we don't have other option.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="gkzfYRVc">反正在下结论的时候（哪怕明明是很确定的事情)，记得加一个seems, appears，语气没那么生硬。</p>
 <p data-pid="z8xRazrH">另外，描述一个东西，就算是明显已经知道很好，也要说looks good, sounds good，而不是It's good.</p>
 <p data-pid="Kak6LYsK">以下还要注意的几点</p>
 <p data-pid="YJjIk9k8"><b>1）不要总是说maybe这种垃圾话，如果确定性比较大的情况下，用probably（实在不行改成seem也好）</b></p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="RFRdxSlp">比较</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="6j4rcrN0">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="rOObfTrf">
  Maybe this is right
  <br><b>This is probably right.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="-m_fAN9w">例二</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="SB6yIlGP">
  Could we have a better way to do it?
  <br>
  Maybe(maybe你妹）.
  <br><b>Probably.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="p9kGLAKE">中国人滥用maybe，不管什么情况都用maybe，给人一种毫无把握的感觉。在表示很有可能的时候，老外都用probably。maybe是比较不确定。《纸牌屋》里Peter Russo和他家乡的人说关闭了船厂，但是他有一项新的法案maybe可以让他们重新获得工作，结果这个maybe惹怒了他家乡人。</p>
 <p data-pid="An7wAgBQ">如果想表示“我很可能是错的”用I'm probably wrong.</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="g46HwNMO"><b>2）给别人提供建议时，不要用should，也不要you'd better，而是用It would be better</b></p>
 <p data-pid="6rQPAuGi">比较</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="m_JXhnQf">
  This design should be changed.
  <br><b>It would be better if we can change the design.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="FkgUlVkO"><b>3）表示一件事希望你做，但是不强求时，用It would be nice/great 而不是 I need you to xxx, I hope you xxxx, could you?</b></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="0zoaMDgk">
  I hope you can come today. 
  <br>
  You need to come today.
  <br>
  Could you come today?
  <br>
  I<b>t would be nice/great if you can come today. (隐含的意思是still OK if you can't make it.)</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="89Y_wmDi">再举个例子</p>
 <p data-pid="COLCkiJG">比较</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="lW3v7ET8">
  Can you bring some wine to the party?
  <br><b>It would be nice/great if you can bring some wine to the party.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="idAEI5t1"><b>4）问别人你要东西/ 信息时，用May I have...? 而不要Could you give me?</b></p>
 <p data-pid="YBv6PE1r">比较</p>
 <p data-pid="0lE2w7ua">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="l1KzXjaO">
  What's your name?
  <br><b>May I have your name?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="pM46CJoz">例二</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="atU3yPGY">
  Could you send me the drawing today?
  <br><b>May I have the drawing today?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="JNMV_f57">例三 《血战钢锯岭》里，法官要老Doss把那封信给他，他是说 May I have the letter ？而不是Could you give me the letter?</p>
 <p data-pid="kNO_o4kw">例四 问别人有没有时间</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="zl7F1Go0">
  <b>Could I have a minute/moment of your time?（非常非常礼貌，销售员问法）</b>
  <br><b>Do you have a minute/moment??（一般礼貌，同事朋友之间问法）</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="eE-cabYy">例五 你比如你想独处一下，比如想打个私人电话，要别人回避一下。</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="DTcRM4dg">
  Could you come out?
  <br><b>Could I have a moment(for myself)?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="IGS2yTGg"><b>5) 问别人是否可以做一件事时，用 Is it possible that...比Could you ..更礼貌</b></p>
 <p data-pid="elBoEl-g">比较</p>
 <p data-pid="W_HKRPFj">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="U_o3nYFQ">
  Could you change the design?
  <br><b>Is it possible for you to change the design?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="RH9VRciW">例二</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="-ffNkKdF">
  Could you call me tomorrow?
  <br><b>Is it possible for you to call tomorrow?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="8HpStuf9"><b>6) 请别人帮忙做一件事时，在前面加(by) any chance，意思是如果有时间/有机会，很委婉。</b></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="LnPr3dpZ">
  By any chance, could you help check the data? 有时间可不可以帮忙看看数据？
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="CZLkDl1y"><b>7) 指责别人时候，用现在进行时比一般现在时要更委婉。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="R5tj0Rh1">比较</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="pmiSIVDP">
  你很没耐心/强词夺理/无理取闹
  <br>
  You are impatient/argumentative/unreasonable
  <br><b>You are being impatient//argumentative/unreasonable</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="VuRIi7Ur">说前者会得罪很多人，因为你用一个 are 就把别人完全说死了，正常的说法是用being，这样表示某时某刻这个人处于的状态,不表示你把他彻底说死。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="vmFnHDUh"><b>8) 表达要求时多用进行时。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="8aA1LsjP">比较</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="X46eftNp">
  We need to do this as soon as possible
  <br><b>We need to be doing this as soon as possible.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="EgF4NHXs">老外普遍用后者用的多。</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="S_KVWJek">
  《纸牌屋》里，Clair的母亲对她的一帮朋友说我们要支持Clair，说的就是We should be supporting her, 而不是We should support her.
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="YRuKZ6-p">以上两种情况中，如果不用进行时，就有表示永久去做这件事的意思，用进行时就可以委婉表示我们会进入这种状况，不是永久的。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="2gt54U65"><b>9) 用would like 代替want吧</b></p>
 <p data-pid="8RKzymbX">中国人用 want比较多，但是want稍微带要求的意思，用would like就委婉一点。</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="6aZlMMqj">
  我想要和你开个会。
  <br>
  I want to have a meeting with you.
  <br><b>I'd like to have a meeting with you.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="6s3CuAn7"><b>10) 用过去式表示一种委婉语气</b></p>
 <p data-pid="xT1DBCJw">口语中经常用过去式表示一种委婉语气，比如 I wanted, I<br>
  thought, I was thinking, I was wondering, I was hoping 其实说话留下余地，因为这是表示过去的状态，没有说明现在的状况，从而形成一种委婉。很多人误以为thought就是"以为”用来表示过去的错误想法，其实不是，只是表示过去的状况，不一定是和现在相反。thought一样可以理解成 “认为”.</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="bPD2O97K"><b>11) 慎用please</b></p>
 <p data-pid="V6F-3Qry">please有时候稍微带一点要求命令的语气，特别是在给客户或者老板写邮件请求某件事，把please替换成疑问句。</p>
 <p data-pid="VXT4f0DG">比较</p>
 <p data-pid="c6L1Py-V">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="JJtLgKqi">
  Please reply to me by today.
  <br><b>Could you reply to me by today?</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="gBWAaWy6">而以下情况下用please就没问题，因为有let me</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="0ujmUmVJ">
  Please let me know your feedback.
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="rVXfzuSK"><b>12) 用let, allow更礼貌</b></p>
 <p data-pid="Qw1e-uLw">比较</p>
 <p data-pid="OTfPU9H1">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="gFL7N5xu">
  Please tell me your feedback.
  <br><b>Please let me know your feedback.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="9KviGmC5">例二</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="vZguDUOF">
  I want to make a proposal
  <br><b>Please allow me to make a proposal.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="BKw3gt64"><b>13)不要不分场合乱用screw, suck 这些非正式用语。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="c6GAri1J">我专门写过一篇文章讨论，吐槽鲁豫在节目中用screw up这个词</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="kKBzTz66">
  If you screw this up, you will not have a second chance.
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="co9grlHY"><a href="https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/24030595" class="internal">英语 | 到底什么是正式场合和非正式场合</a></p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="SjFyXMMZ"><b>14) 由于客观上导致做不到，not able to 代替 cannot </b></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="fqFKlYIZ">
  Sorry I can't come to the party today since I'll need to work very late.
  <br><b>Sorry I'm not able to come to the party today since I'll need to work very late.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="AmRCoRS3"><b>15) 给need 加将来时，更加委婉。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="K4ssNCrg"><b>比较</b></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="9pjz7Tlv">
  You need to buy a new machine if this one doesn't work
  <br><b>You'll need to buy a new machine if this one doesn't work.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="pKo9YPOV"><b>16) 美国人特别喜欢用I guess代替I think表达委婉的语气。</b></p>
 <p data-pid="im-krqsq"><b>比较</b></p>
 <p data-pid="NySqVRH2">例一</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="cTjTb7vB">
  I think we'll leave now.
  <br><b>I guess we'll leave now.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="3mNN5xGA">例二</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="Dp40Qqro">
  I think we can do better.
  <br><b>I guess we can do better.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="FeiWQ9BD"><b>17) 用I'm good代替no</b></p>
 <p data-pid="ZTxtOreA">中国习惯yes or no式的思维。但是有些场合回答no未免有点唐突，美国人一般用I'm good来婉拒（帮助或者好意），等于说I'm feeling good/I'm satisfied and don't need anything.</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="9-1NrkFZ">
  <b>Would you care for some coffee?</b> <b>I'm good. Thank you.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="VL56b0T8">详情请参见芬哥这篇文章</p>
 <h2><a href="https://www.zhihu.com/question/20588106/answer/150928053" class="internal">英文中，有哪些婉转礼貌的方法表示「否认」与「拒绝」？</a></h2>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="X9qfINLR"><b>18） 指责别人用You are better than that.</b></p>
 <p data-pid="za0anXVU">指出别人错误的时候，一般不说you are wrong/careless/weak, 一般说you are better than that，这是非常常见而且礼貌的说法。</p>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="n0oL6mnN"><b>19） 某些负面情况下用have difficulty 代替 can't，委婉把责任推向自己这边</b></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="8qVkfU86">
  I can't trust you.
  <br><b>I have difficulty trusting you.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <blockquote data-pid="P6rKrUod">
  I don't understand your question.
  <br><b>I have difficulty understanding your question.</b>
 </blockquote>
 <p class="ztext-empty-paragraph"><br></p>
 <p data-pid="Gsrasxyk"><b>20)</b> <b>委婉命令别人做某事，用it might be a good idea比 please 要礼貌</b>《萨利机长》里空姐要求乘客把安全带系上，说的是</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="TmSoI2_P">
  <b>It might be a good idea to fasten your seat belt.</b>
 </blockquote>
