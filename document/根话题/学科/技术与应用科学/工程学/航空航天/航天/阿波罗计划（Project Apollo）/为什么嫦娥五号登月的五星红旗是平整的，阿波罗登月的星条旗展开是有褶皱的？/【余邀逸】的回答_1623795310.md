# 为什么嫦娥五号登月的五星红旗是平整的，阿波罗登月的星条旗展开是有褶皱的？
- 点赞数：5332

<body>
 <p data-pid="bBOvRYkY">美国登月的旗子是就是普通尼龙做的，5.5美刀一面。而这个尼龙是聚酰胺纤维（锦纶）的一种说法。通俗的说，尼龙就是一种塑料。</p>
 <p data-pid="k4AZpnsv">打个不恰当的比方，美国国旗就好比是塑料袋，发生塑性形变后不会自动复原。而从发射到登月过程中又没有好好保管，放到发射仓里是一坨，拿出来是一坨，插到月球上还是那一坨，所以看起来有褶皱。</p>
 <p data-pid="VyUjbhBz">事实上在阿波罗登月计划中每次登月都会插旗子，到现在载人登月六次，月球上插了五面旗子。最出名的那面，也就是阿波罗11号阿姆斯特朗插的，因为离返回舱太近被引擎排气吹翻了，所以现在只剩五面在月球上。</p>

 <p data-pid="mH4piy9d">首先看第一段话：</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="cN4sg80H">
  Reichhardt, Tony, <a href="https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//www.airspacemag.com/space-exploration/Finding_Apollo.html" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Finding Apollo</a>, Air and Space, Smithsonian Institution, September 2008.
  <br>
  <br><i>The (Apollo 11’s) flag is probably gone. Buzz Aldrin saw it knocked over by the rocket blast as he and Neil Armstrong left the moon 39 summers ago. Lying there in the lunar dust, unprotected from the sun’s harsh ultraviolet rays, the flag’s red and blue would have bleached white in no time. Over the years, the nylon would have turned brittle and disintegrated. … Dennis Lacarrubba, whose New Jersey-based company, Annin, made the flag and sold it to NASA for $5.50 in 1969, considers what might happen to an ordinary nylon flag left outside for 39 years on Earth, let alone on the moon. He thinks for a few seconds. “I can’t believe there would be anything left,” he concludes. “I gotta be honest with you. It’s gonna be ashes.”</i>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="pAtWswG9">翻译一下：</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="I_0ERTmi">
  赖克哈特托尼<a href="https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//www.airspacemag.com/space-exploration/Finding_Apollo.html" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">寻找阿波罗</a>，“航空与航天”，史密森学会，2008年9月。
  <br><i>阿波罗11号的旗帜可能已经消失了。巴兹·奥尔德林在39年夏天和尼尔·阿姆斯特朗离开月球时，看到它被火箭冲击击倒。躺在那里的月球尘埃，不受太阳严酷的紫外线的保护，旗帜的红色和蓝色将在短期内漂成白色。多年来，尼龙会变得易碎和崩解。…丹尼斯拉卡拉瓦，他的新泽西公司，安宁，制造了这面旗帜，并在1969年以5.50美元的价格卖给了美国宇航局，他认为在地球上放置39年的普通尼龙旗帜可能会发生什么变化，更不用说在月球上了。他想了几秒钟后说。“我真不敢相信还会留下来什么东西，”他总结道。“我得跟你说实话。它会变成灰烬的。“</i>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="2jUrdEcA">然后看最后一段：</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="oMHpvNdi">
  Finally, Lunar Scientist Paul Spudis, in a <a href="https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//blogs.airspacemag.com/moon/2011/07/faded-flags-on-the-moon/" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">July 2011 blog posting</a>, writes:
  <br>
  <br><i>For forty-odd years, the flags have been exposed to the full fury of the Moon’s environment – alternating 14 days of searing sunlight and 100° C heat with 14 days of numbing-cold -150° C darkness. But even more damaging is the intense ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the pure unfiltered sunlight on the cloth (</i><a href="https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//www.wisegeek.com/what-is-modal.htm" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">modal</a><i>) from which the Apollo flags were made. Even on Earth, the colors of a cloth flag flown in bright sunlight for many years will eventually fade and need to be replaced. So it is likely that these symbols of American achievement have been rendered blank, bleached white by the UV radiation of unfiltered sunlight on the lunar surface. Some of them may even have begun to physically disintegrate under the intense flux.</i>
 </blockquote>
 <p data-pid="xwEBvS_5">翻译一下：</p>
 <blockquote data-pid="h0QVvGGF">
  最后，月球科学家保罗·斯普迪斯<a href="https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//blogs.airspacemag.com/moon/2011/07/faded-flags-on-the-moon/" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">2011年7月博客发帖</a>，写到：
  <br>
  <br><i>四十多年来，这些旗帜一直暴露在月球严酷的环境下--日照灼热14天，温度100℃，黑暗14天，冷得麻木，-150摄氏度。但更有害的是，太阳光未经过滤，紫外线(UV)辐射直接照在用来制作阿波罗旗帜的布料上。即使在地球上，多年来在明媚的阳光下飘扬的布旗最终也会褪色，需要更换。因此，这些美国成就的象征很可能被月球表面未经过滤的阳光的紫外线辐射而变成空白，漂白成白色。他们中的一些甚至可能已经开始在强烈的热流中分解。</i>
 </blockquote>
